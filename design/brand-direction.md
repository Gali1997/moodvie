# Moodvie — Brand Direction

## The one-liner
A private movie theater in your browser that reads how you feel and hands you one film.

## Mood & feeling
Late-night cinema. The kind of small independent theater where the usher knows your name and the seats are velvet. Dark, warm, intimate — not slick, not corporate. This is not Netflix. This is not a list of 40 options. It's one film, chosen for you, like a friend who just knows.

The design should feel like stepping into a room that was set up just for you. Gold light on dark walls. Film grain in the air. The smell of popcorn and old wood.

## Who is this for?
People who are tired of scrolling. People who open a streaming app, spend 20 minutes browsing, and end up rewatching The Office. People who want someone to just *pick* for them — but pick well, and tell them why.

## What it is NOT
- Not a search engine. No filters, no genres, no "top 10 this week."
- Not a chatbot. You don't need to "prompt engineer" your request.
- Not flashy or gamified. No streaks, no achievements, no social feed.
- Not a spreadsheet of options. One mood in, one film out.

## Visual references
Sites that inspired the feel (not the function):
- **sloshseltzer.com** — dramatic scroll choreography, massive typography, premium product feel from a simple product
- **buttermax.net** — custom WebGL, cinematic transitions, every pixel is intentional
- **chirpley.ai** — bold 3D, interactive hero, makes you want to scroll
- **scroll-driven-animations.style** — shows what's possible with scroll alone

## Design principles

### 1. Cinema, not interface
Every element should feel like it belongs in a movie theater — ticket stubs, curtains, audience silhouettes, film grain. The metaphor is consistent and never breaks.

### 2. Less is the whole point
The product does one thing. The design should too. One input. One output. White space is not empty — it's breathing room.

### 3. Gold, not neon
Premium means restraint. Gold accents on deep black. Warm cream text, not white. The palette is small and every color earns its place.

### 4. Movement with purpose
Animation exists to create a feeling, not to show off. The curtain opening, the ticket unfolding, the poster tilting — each one makes you feel like something is *happening*. No animation is decorative.

### 5. Personal, not corporate
This was made by one person. It should feel handmade. The copy is conversational. The about page is a personal letter. The footer says "made by Galina" not "© 2026 Moodvie Inc."

## Tone of voice
- Talk like a friend who knows a lot about film but doesn't lecture
- Short sentences. Direct. Warm but not cute.
- Ok to be poetic ("the right film for this exact moment") but never precious
- Never say "curated" or "personalized experience" or "discover"
- It's "film" not "content." It's "mood" not "preference."
- The word "picture" was tested and rejected — too pretentious

## One word that captures it
**Intimate.**
